Purpose

To provide administrators a procedure in hiring foreign nationals.

Policy

This policy complies with the University's Policy on Hiring Staff and Faculty, in the Human Resources Chapter of the Handbook of Operating Procedures. A request to hire a foreign national is made by a University Administrator.

Procedures

  1. The determination as to what type of visa better fits the foreign national and the short-term needs of the hiring department is made between the hiring official, Human Resource Services (HRS), and the International Student and Scholar Services (ISSS) Department at the University of Texas at Austin.
  2. In the event, the hiring official intends to employ the foreign national for longer than one (1) year, a temporary specialty worker visas (H1-B) visa may be requested. The H-1B visa is used for the temporary employment of a worker in a specialty occupation.
  3. The basic requirements are that the position demands the services of an individual with at least a baccalaureate level of education in a specific field, and that the employee holds the relevant degree or equivalent experience. The H-1B visa may be requested for an initial period of three (3) years with a three (3) year extension available.
  4. The current application processing time is approximately four (4) to six (6) months including time for the educational evaluation and the labor condition application. Unless the petition is filed through the premium processing route. The expedited application processing time under this method is approximately two (2) to three (3) weeks.
  5. The University, through the hiring department and/or College will pay all associated fees related to the filing of an H1-B petition for faculty, research, and selected staff positions. Information as to what position titles fall under this procedure is available through HRS. Payment of the premium processing fee by the University is subject to certain conditions.
  6. The hiring official will work with HRS and ISSS to process and file the petition with the United States Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) according to specified wage and posting guidelines. Upon approval, such notice will be sent by the USCIS to the designated U.S. Consulate abroad where the employee would present his/her passport for issuance of the visa. Or, if the employee is eligible he/she may change status in the United States.
  7. The University will maintain a separate public examination file. Information as to what constitutes the "public examination" file is available through HRS.
  8. In some cases, depending on the purpose and type of visa, the hiring official may be referred to the Office of International Programs (OIP) for student (F-1) and exchange visitor (J-1) visas for assistance.

Applicability

Faculty, Research, and selected staff position titles.

Definitions

  • F1 - Student visa
  • HRS - Human Resource Services
  • H1-B - Three (3) year specialty worker visa, renewable up to six (6) years
  • ISSS - International Student and Scholar Services, UT Austin
  • J1 - Exchange visitor visa
  • OIP - Office of International Programs
  • USCIS - United States Citizenship and Immigration Services

Responsible Party

Director, Human Resource Services
