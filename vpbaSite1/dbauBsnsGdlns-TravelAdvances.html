Purpose

The University may advance funds to its employees for projected travel expenses when traveling on official state business.

Policy

In order to receive a cash (travel) advance, the University requires an approved Travel Authorization and a promissory note (Authorization to Deduct Outstanding Travel Advance from Payroll Check). The state of Texas allows a travel advance for up to 90% of the per diem for meals and lodging.

Travel advance recipients will be allowed 30 days from the completion of the travel to redeposit unused travel advance funds to the Student Business Services Office. Additional travel advances will not be issued if an outstanding travel advance is still pending.

As a reminder, the University follows Internal Revenue Service (IRS) regulations, which allow an accountable plan to be non-taxable. Any travel advance that is not substantiated within 60 days of the date issued will be added to the employee's income and taxed at the appropriate withholding tax rate. Refer: IRS Publication 463, Section 6 How to Report.

Procedures

The cash (travel) advance request must be submitted to the Accounts Payable/Travel department at least 7 days in advance to ensure approval before the trip. Emergency checks will not be issued for late requests. The amount requested cannot exceed 90% of the per diem for meals and lodging or 90% of the per diem for meals, if lodging is prepaid.

A travel advance may be requested by contacting the department travel planner. The department travel planner will initiate the travel advance request. The request document must contain the following information:

  • Amount - 90% of meals and lodging (if lodging is prepaid, then only 90% of meals)
  • Travel Authorization Number should be noted in comments field

The following documentation must be submitted to Accounts Payable/Travel via the BPLogix form:

  • Approved Travel Authorization
  • Promissory note with traveler's certification

All receipts should be submitted to the Accounts Payable/Travel department to reconcile the travel advance after the trip takes place. Repayment of unused or excess travel advance funds must be deposited at the Student Business Services Office within 30 days.

Applicability

Applies to all University faculty and staff requesting a travel advance, if any of the following are true:

  • The destination of the travel is to an isolated region with limited access to suppliers or services that accept credit cards
  • Travel is for an extended period of time. (more than 3 days)
  • Traveler does not hold a UTEP Citibank Individual Travel Card
  • A faculty member is taking a group of students
  • Prior travel advances are not outstanding more than 30 days

Responsible Party

Comptroller
