Other Leave Programs

You are here: Business Process Guidelines » Other Leave Programs

Download Printer Friendly Version

Purpose

To provide eligible employees the ability and process to use other approved paid leave programs.

Policy

This policy provides for administration of other paid leave benefits (i.e. jury duty, emergency and funeral leave) for eligible employees of The University of Texas at El Paso.

Procedures

Funeral Leave:

Employees wishing to use funeral leave must request leave and receive approval with their respective supervisor upon notification of an immediate family member's death. Leave may not be granted for more than three (3) work days.

In the event an employee requires additional time to attend to funeral matters, the employee can request additional leave using their paid vacation time.

The employee must submit a leave request form indicating the period of funeral leave.

Emergency Leave:

An employee may be granted emergency leave for other reasons determined to be for good cause, with the required approval.

Supervisory approval is required before the leave is taken. The employee must submit a leave request form indicating the period of leave.

In some cases, evidence of leave may be requested by the University.

Jury Duty and Witness Service:

In the event an employee is notified for jury service, an employee should notify the supervisor as soon as notice of jury service is received. The employee must furnish documentary proof of service to the immediate supervisor.

The employee must submit a leave request form indicating the period of jury duty leave.

Information on witness service is available through the University's Handbook of Operating Procedures or the Human Resource Services (HRS) Office.

Applicability

An employee of The University of Texas at El Paso who is appointed to work at least twenty hours per week for a period of four and one-half months or more in a position that does not require student status as a condition of employment will be granted bereavement leave for a death in the employee’s family.

Definitions

Employee’s family - is defined for these purposes as the employee's spouse, or the parent, brother, sister, grandparent, grandchild, or child of the employee or the employee's spouse.

Responsible Party

Director, Human Resource Services
