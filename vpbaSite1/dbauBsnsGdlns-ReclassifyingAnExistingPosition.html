Reclassifying an Existing Position

Purpose

To outline the procedure to reclassify an existing position which includes job audits and market analysis.

Policy

A reclassification of a position is necessary when its duties have changed sufficiently to require a change of title. A reclassification always involves a change in title, but it may not necessarily involve a change in salary. Handbook of Operating Procedures, Chapter 2, Section 2.5

Procedures

  1. The Director/Manager completes a Reclassification/Audit Request Form including appropriate signatures.
  2. The Director/Manager updates/revises the current position description and forwards to HR Services.
  3. Human Resource Services will review/analyze and make recommendations to the Director/Manager who initiated the request.
  4. If HR recommends a job title change only, the recommendation must be approved by requestor and Dean/VP, then sent to HR Services for appropriate change to be made in the job code system. Upon change in job code system, HR notifies offices of VPBA, Budget, HR Services and requestor.
  5. If HR recommends a change in minimum salary and/or salary range, HR will assess and analyze impact of such change to department and the university as a whole and notify requestor of potential financial impact to budget for further approval as required.
  6. After all approvals have been obtained, requester discusses results with affected employee(s) and initiates BIS appointment with changes.

Applicability

Applies to all University departments.

Responsible Party

Director, Human Resource Services
