Overtime Guidance

The University complies with the Federal Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), applicable state laws, and the Texas State Comptroller's Payroll Policies and Procedures Guide in determining who is entitled to be compensated for overtime worked in excess of the normal 40-hour work week.

What Hours are Not Considered Overtime and Who Qualifies?

Any type of paid leave (i.e., vacation or sick leave), holidays taken during a work week cannot be counted as actual time worked or towards calculating the number of overtime hours worked. Only employees classified as non-exempt qualify to be paid for authorized overtime worked.

Who Can Authorize and Approve Overtime?

The approval must be from the department supervisor prior to any overtime worked by non-exempt employees. Departments are solely responsible to maintain adequate paper documentation detailing the overtime worked.

Processing Procedures

  1. Departments must create an electronic (OV5) Department Overtime Pay Document to pay employees that have worked overtime.
  2. Department must enter the overtime hours worked at 1.5 times.
    1. Example: For an employee who worked a total of 45 hours in a week:
      1. 45 (actual hours worked) - 40 (regular hour work week) = 5 hours over
      2. 5 (hours over) X 1.5 (overtime rate) = 7.5 (overtime hours) entered in OV5
  3. Department justification for overtime
    1. Department needs to enter an explanation for the overtime in the notes section of the OV5 document
    2. Payroll Office will not approve an OV5 without the attached notes on the document
  4. Department needs to route OV5 document for approval
  5. Department must process the OV5 by the timesheet deadline posted on the Administrative Calendar.
  6. Overtime will only occur on the two semi-monthly payrolls (i.e., 5th and 15th working day of the month). Emergency checks are not issued for overtime payments.
