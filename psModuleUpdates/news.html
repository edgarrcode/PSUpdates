New Payroll Transactions Query Available!

October 17, 2014 | Back to top

In order to assist you in reconciling accounts, the UTE_BUDGET_TRANSACTIONS_PAYROL query is now available in PeopleSoft. This query includes information on payroll transaction amounts, reference data (employee name, EMPL IDs, journal numbers), and payment type (classified, A&P, student, overtime, etc.). Complete the following steps in PeopleSoft in order to run the query:

  1. Go to Main Menu>FMS Reporting Tools>Query>Query Viewer.
  2. In the search box, enter UTE_BUDGET_TRANSACTIONS_PAYROL and hit search.
  3. Run the query in Excel or HTML.
  4. Enter your six digit Department Number.
  5. Search by cost center or project ID.
    1. Searching for one cost center: enter the cost center in the Cost Centr field, and then enter a percent sign (%) in the Project field.
    2. Searching for one project ID: enter the project ID in the Project field, and then enter a percent sign (%) in the Cost Centr field.
    3. Searching for all cost centers/project IDs for your department: enter a percent sign (%) in both the Cost Centr and Project field.
  6. Enter the Budget Year.
    1. Enter the second year of the fiscal year. For example, for FY 2013-2014, enter 2014.
  7. Enter the Budget Period.
    1. The budget period follows the fiscal year accounting period. For example, for May, enter 09. (September=01, October=02, November=03, December=04, January=05, February=06, March=07, April=08, May=09, June=10, July=11, August=12).

Account Reconciliation Process trainings will now include a review of the new payroll transactions query. If you are interested in attending a training, visit the PeopleSoft Training Calendar for new training dates and times.

“Need to Knows” for Processing Travel & Vendor Payments

May 15, 2014 | Back to top

Correlating IAP Forms with PeopleSoft Documents

As you know, information in IAP forms needs to match information that is entered in PeopleSoft. IAP forms for travel authorizations, employee reimbursements, and vendor payments all have a unique system-generated number called a DTN. To help us correlate PeopleSoft documents with their respective IAP forms, please include the IAP DTN when you enter a transaction in PeopleSoft. Quick guides are available for locating and adding DTN information for Vendor Payments, Travel Authorizations, and Employee Reimbursements.

Expense Reimbursements

Itemizing Employee Reimbursements

To add multiple line items in the Employee Reimbursement IAP form, click on the “Add Line Item” button. Remember, line items and cost center/project IDs in the IAP form need to match line items that are entered in PeopleSoft.

Rounding Travel Mileage

When entering travel mileage in either the IAP form or PeopleSoft, round up to the nearest whole mile. For example, if the traveler traveled 23.3 miles, round up to 24 miles.

Travel Authorizations

Airfare

When creating a travel authorization through the IAP form or through PeopleSoft, do not include airfare charges. Airfare is charged directly through the University’s ghost card, and allocated through the Travel Office.

Payment Type

When creating a travel authorization in PeopleSoft, always select “Paid By Employee” for the Payment Type under the Details section.

Entering Authorization IDs in Concur

The Authorization ID that needs to be entered in Concur is made up of two parts: TV (travel authorization type) followed by the Authorization ID generated in PeopleSoft. Do not include any extra spaces between the travel authorization type and the PeopleSoft Authorization ID. The following is an example of what the Authorization ID in Concur would look like: TV0000002609.

Grant Projects Available for HR/Finance Transaction Processing

May 12, 2014 | Back to top

Grant projects are now available for processing HR and Finance transactions! You may now begin to process all pending HR and Finance transactions such as hires, travel documents, vendor expenses, budget transfer, purchase orders, etc.

Please keep the following points in mind during this transitional period:

  • As with any other project or cost center (formerly “account” in DEFINE), the expenses in Budget Overview are as of March 2014. April expenses will be converted to PeopleSoft by mid-month. Be conservative with purchases to reduce the risk of overspending your grants budget.
  • There are new grants budget items in PeopleSoft; though they may appear differently from DEFINE, the total budget is the same.
  • If you have questions on the mapping of expense types to new budget items, please view the Grants Define Object Codes crosswalk in our PeopleSoft Training Documents page (under the Grants section).

As a reminder, all approvals need to be collected through interim approval processes before the transaction can be processed in PeopleSoft. If you need assistance processing HR or Finance transactions, contact the Help Desk by emailing helpdesk@utep.edu, or calling extension 4357 (off-campus: 747-5257). By submitting your inquiries through this avenue, you will be helping us track common issues.

Do You Process HR and Finance Transactions?

May 6, 2014 | Back to top

When PeopleSoft goes live, interim approval processes will be put in place to collect approvals for common HR and Finance transactions. Departments will initiate the collection of approvals through either a paper or electronic method outside of the PeopleSoft system.

Entering Transactions in PeopleSoft

After approvals are collected outside of PeopleSoft, the department may also be responsible for entering the transaction into PeopleSoft. Note: Not all transactions will be entered in PeopleSoft by the department; certain transactions will be entered by the central processing units (Human Resources, Payroll, Budget, etc.).
