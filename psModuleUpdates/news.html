New Hire/Rehire IAP Form New Functionality

September 2, 2014 | Back to top

In order to confirm that an employee funded through a grant has sufficient funding for the duration of their appointment, new fields were added to the New Hire/Rehire IAP form. When a grant funding source is entered, project begin/end date fields will now auto-populate. If the funding source begin and end dates that you enter do not fall within the project begin/end date timeframe, a different or additional funding source must be added to fund the remaining duration of the appointment.

For example, Paydirt Pete’s appointment is from 9/1/14 to 5/31/15. If the grant that funds his appointment ends on 12/31/14, a second funding source must be entered to fund the 1/1/15 to 5/31/15 period. Below is an example of how his funding would be assigned:

Funding Period Funding Source
9/1/14 — 12/31/14 226XXXXXXA
1/1/15 — 5/31/15 14XXXXXX

Note: The new fields were also added to the Position Funding Changes IAP form.

Changing the Preferred Payment Date When Creating a Non-PO Voucher in PeopleSoft

August 28, 2014 | Back to top

In order to change the preferred payment date for a non-PO voucher created in PeopleSoft, fill out the required voucher fields, then complete the following steps:

  1. Go to the “Payments” tab.
  2. Update the preferred payment date in the “Scheduled Due” field.

Note: All non-PO vouchers created in PeopleSoft default to pay 30 days from either the entered Invoice Receipt Date or the entered Goods/Service Receipt Date; whichever is the later date.

Extending or Hiring Employees for the Fall Semester

August 22, 2014 | Back to top

In order to ensure that all hourly employees have an active appointment for the fall semester and can receive their first paycheck on September 17, please process their New Hire/Rehire IAP form no later than September 5 at 5:00 p.m.

To identify your hourly employees with an 08/31/14 end date in PeopleSoft:

  1. Go to Main Menu>Workforce Administration>Job Information>Current Job
  2. Search for your hourly employee.
  3. Under the Work Location tab, view the Expected Job End Date field.

After you have identified hourly employees with an 08/31/14 end date, review with management to determine which employees need to have their appointment extended. When completing the New Hire/Rehire IAP form, make sure to enter 09/01/14 as the new start date. New Hire/Rehire IAP forms need to have account owner approval by September 5 at 5:00 p.m.

Note: If you have already processed IAP forms to hire or extend appointments for hourly employees for the fall, you do not need to do so again.

PeopleSoft Cascading

August 11, 2014 | Back to top

Cascading refers to an action that the PeopleSoft system takes when the amount of leave time that is submitted for an employee is more than what the employee has available. The system will automatically “cascade,” and attempt to pull leave from another leave type to compensate for the difference.

In collaboration with our partners and UT Share, the decision was made to not include Compensation Time and Overtime in the cascading order for Vacation, Educational Activities, and Sick leave in PeopleSoft.

Accessing Your Electronic Earnings Statement in PeopleSoft

June 2, 2014 | Back to top

If you are an employee paid on a monthly basis, you can view your electronic earnings statement (paycheck stub) in PeopleSoft on the first business day of the month. To view your electronic earnings statement:

  1. Go to the PeopleSoft Login site.
  2. In the search box, enter “The University of Texas at El Paso” and click “Continue.”
  3. Login with your UTEP email credentials (username and password).
  4. Under the Employee Self Service section, click “View Paycheck.”
  5. Click the paycheck date under Check Date.

If your earnings statement does not open, you may need to disable pop up blockers.

You will no longer receive an email notification when the payroll earnings statement is available. To view your earnings statement in the future, you can log in to PeopleSoft through the login button on the Home page, or through the PeopleSoft link on my.utep.edu.

Collecting Approvals & Entering Transactions in PeopleSoft

May 16, 2014 | Back to top

Currently there is no functionality to route a document for approval in PeopleSoft. For this reason, approvals for common HR and Finance transactions have to be collected outside of PeopleSoft before the transaction can be entered in PeopleSoft. In order to get your transaction processed, the following steps have to be taken:

Step 1: Initiate an interim approval process (IAP) form to collect data and approvals for a transaction.

Step 2: Once all approvals have been collected, the transaction can be entered in PeopleSoft.

Remember, initiating an IAP form will not automatically populate data in PeopleSoft. This step needs to be done by the department or a central processing unit (i.e. Accounts Payable/Travel, HR, etc.), depending on the type of transaction. To see who is responsible for entering HR and Finance transactions in PeopleSoft, click here.

Correlating IAP Forms with PeopleSoft Documents

For internal control purposes, central processing units cannot final approve a transaction entered in PeopleSoft unless it matches information in an IAP form. Critical information such as accounting details, amounts, dates, itemized line items, etc. should be the same in both documents. If data does not match, transactions cannot be processed.

“Need to Knows” for Processing Travel & Vendor Payments

May 15, 2014 | Back to top

Correlating IAP Forms with PeopleSoft Documents

As you know, information in IAP forms needs to match information that is entered in PeopleSoft. IAP forms for travel authorizations, employee reimbursements, and vendor payments all have a unique system-generated number called a DTN. To help us correlate PeopleSoft documents with their respective IAP forms, please include the IAP DTN when you enter a transaction in PeopleSoft. Quick guides are available for locating and adding DTN information for Vendor Payments, Travel Authorizations, and Employee Reimbursements.

Expense Reimbursements

Itemizing Employee Reimbursements

To add multiple line items in the Employee Reimbursement IAP form, click on the “Add Line Item” button. Remember, line items and cost center/project IDs in the IAP form need to match line items that are entered in PeopleSoft.

Rounding Travel Mileage

When entering travel mileage in either the IAP form or PeopleSoft, round up to the nearest whole mile. For example, if the traveler traveled 23.3 miles, round up to 24 miles.

Travel Authorizations

Airfare

When creating a travel authorization through the IAP form or through PeopleSoft, do not include airfare charges. Airfare is charged directly through the University’s ghost card, and allocated through the Travel Office.

Payment Type

When creating a travel authorization in PeopleSoft, always select “Paid By Employee” for the Payment Type under the Details section.

Entering Authorization IDs in Concur

The Authorization ID that needs to be entered in Concur is made up of two parts: TV (travel authorization type) followed by the Authorization ID generated in PeopleSoft. Do not include any extra spaces between the travel authorization type and the PeopleSoft Authorization ID. The following is an example of what the Authorization ID in Concur would look like: TV0000002609.
